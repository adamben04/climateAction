Climate change brought on by global warming poses a serious threat to life on
earth in the form of extreme weather events and widespread flooding. Scientists are still researching global warming and how
it affects the planet. Animals and plants both produce greenhouse gases during respiration and photosynthesis, respectively.




Cuases of Climate Change

Fast Fashion

Fast fashion's drawbacks include the inability to pay workers a fair living wage, poor working conditions, child labor,
environmental damage from hazardous chemicals, materials made of plastic, rising levels of water pollution, and textile waste.

Plastic

Strong greenhouse gases are released by the plastic as a result of heat and sunlight, creating a worrisome feedback loop. The plastic
degrades into more methane and ethylene as the planet heats up due to our changing climate, speeding up the rate at which it changes, and continuing the cycle.




Carbon Emissions

The earth's surface temperature has increased as a result of these greenhouse gas
emissions and the greenhouse effect. More than any other human activity, the burning of fossil fuels
alters the climate. Carbon dioxide: Currently, over 30 billion tons of carbon dioxide are released into the atmosphere annually as a result of human activity.

Poissible Solution

Producing energy from fossil fuels with no greenhouse gas emissions while lowering some forms of air pollution. increasing energy supply diversity
and decreasing reliance on imported fuels. fostering economic growth and employment in manufacturing, installation, and other fields.
